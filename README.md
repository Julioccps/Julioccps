### The Origin Story (Constraint-Driven Development)

My journey into **Systems Programming** and **Compiler Design** wasn't born from abundance, but from limitation.

- **CPU:** Intel Celeron N4000 (1.1 GHz)
- **RAM:** 4GB
- **Storage:** 56GB eMMC

That environment taught me a crucial lesson: **Efficiency is not a luxury; it's a requirement.** Writing a kernel in Real Mode Assembly or a compiler on that hardware forced me to understand every cycle and every byte.

Today, I've upgraded the silicon, but the philosophy remains the same: **Maximum performance, zero bloat.**

### Development Principles

-   **Self-Hosting is the Ultimate Goal:** A tool isn't truly mastered until it can create itself.
-   **Abstraction is a Leaky Faucet:** Understand the layer beneath the one you're working in.
-   **Constraints Breed Creativity:** Given infinite resources, any fool can make something work.
-   **Admiration Comes from Understanding:** To truly appreciate something, you have to look in and out of it.
